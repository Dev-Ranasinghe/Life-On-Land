Mountains

Fold Mountains

  • Formed by the folding of Earth's crust.
  • Created when two tectonic plates collide, causing the crust to fold and form mountains.
  • Examples: Himalayas, Alps, Andes, Rockies.

Fault-Block Mountains

  • Formed by the movement of large crustal blocks along faults.
  • Created when faults or cracks in the Earth's crust force some blocks of rock up and others down.
  • Examples: Sierra Nevada, Harz Mountains.

Volcanic Mountains

  • Formed by volcanic activity.
  • Created when magma from beneath the Earth's crust erupts through the surface and piles up.
  • Examples: Mount Fuji, Mount St. Helens, Mauna Kea, Mount Kilimanjaro.

Dome Mountains

  • Formed by the uplifting of rock layers.
  • Created when magma pushes the crust up but does not erupt onto the surface, causing the overlying rocks to bulge.
  • Examples: Black Hills of South Dakota, Adirondack Mountains.

Plateau Mountains

  • Formed by the erosion of a plateau.
  • Created when a plateau (a flat, elevated area) is worn down by weathering and erosion, leaving behind mountainous regions.
  • Examples: Catskill Mountains.
