Worbarrow Bay
Situated on the Isle of Purbeck in Dorset UK is Worbarrow Bay, a small cove over dramatic cliffs jutting into the English Channel. It’s among the most serene spots on earth and can be accessed by foot alone through the abandoned Tyneham village.
Banff Park
Banff National Park was born in 1885 in the Canadian Rockies and is a household name when it comes to breath-taking mountain sceneries, turquoise lakes such as Lake Louise amongst others in plenty and large numbers of wild animals.
Mount Fuji
Mount Fuji is Japan’s highest mountain which stands at 3776m hence making it as a symbol of environmental beauty besides its cultural function. It’s situated in Honshu Island an explosive volcano which last erupted in 1707.
netherlands
The Netherlands is found in Northwestern Europe and is characterized by its flat landscape, extensive canal systems, tulip fields, and iconic windmills. It's land situated below sea level through the help of complex networks of dams and other water barriers for protection.
Death Valley
One of the hottest and driest places on Earth is Death Valley located in eastern California with a 56.7°C (134°F) highest air temperature record in Furnace Creek. In this desert region salt flats, sand dunes, rugged mountains as well as wildlife that survive because they have adapted themselves to this harsh environment.
